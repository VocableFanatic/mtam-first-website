Languages on Focus:

Rights, Diversity and Curiosities

About

This site offers an in-depth approach to language rights, exploring both their broad definition and their specific application in the Brazilian context. Firstly, let's clarify the fundamental importance of these rights, which aim to safeguard and encourage the use of different languages in different social spheres. In the Brazilian scenario, we highlight the complexity of linguistic diversity, addressing the rights linked to indigenous languages, the Brazilian Sign Language (Libras) and bilingual education, emphasising the legislative relevance of these aspects for an inclusive society. We also explore heritage languages, highlighting how the communities that have chosen Brazil as their home contribute to the national linguistic panorama.

In a more specific analysis, we look at the Okinawan language,its origins in Japan and its resilience in Brazil, recognised by UNESCO as being in danger of dying out. We also highlight local initiatives, such as the free Uchinaaguchi course in São Paulo, highlighting the urgent need for preservation. By inviting visitors to explore the interconnections between language, culture and linguistic rights, our website seeks not only to inform, but also to celebrate the richness and linguistic diversity present in Brazil, highlighting the ongoing efforts to protect and promote it.
